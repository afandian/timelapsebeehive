FAQ

Why?

Becuase I'm curious about how bees sound inside the hive. Becuase I want to know what a bee sounds like if no-one's listening.

Where are all the sound recordings?

This project is in its very early stages. There's no data yet, but it's days away. Hopefully by mid-June 2015 there will be!

Do you have a blog?

Yes, my blog is here with some more personal reflections.

Technology?

A custom-written piece of software runs on an Android device (Nexus 7 tablet just because I happened to have one). It uploads recordings to a web server (written in Clojure). This then stores samples and splices them together. The splicing and spectrograms is done with the excellent SoX.

Open source?

Yes, everything. The Android application is Time Lapse Beehive Recorder. The server is Time Lapse Beehive.

What about bees and mobile phones?

There's a highly criticised paper that says mobile phones damage bees' ability to navigate. It's pretty much discredited. That said, I was cautious about putting a mobile phone right up against a beehive. So my routine is to unplug the tablet, take it home and upload over wifi.

If you want to run this software, it could be easily changed to upload over a mobile connection if you wanted.

Where does the poetic name 'Timelapse Beehive Recorder' come from? What inspired it?

It's English. I went through a lot of Latin and Greek words, but you can't beat prosaic, obvious words. It seemed to work for FolkTuneFinder

I want to record my bees!

Please do! Email me at joe@afandian.com and we can discuss. This site is just waiting for more people to upload stuff.

I want to take timelapse recordings, but not of bees.

The Android application doesn't have to be used in a beehive. You can get the wav files off the device like any other file. Mail me at joe@afandian.com to discuss.
